A degree certificate is the official permanent certificate you get after completing your college degree. A provisional certificate is a temporary copy. It is useful for proving that you have completed your degree until you receive the official copy.


A provisional certificate serves as a temporary copy of the official degree, but some institutions and companies may ask for additional documents for certificate verification. This may include proof of identity or a student ID card, depending on the organisation's verification procedure. Most organisations are tolerant when it comes to using provisional certificates for academic background verification.


Yes, you can use a provisional certificate to apply for government jobs. Make sure that your provisional certificate is no older than six months. You are also required to submit the official degree within six months of submitting the provisional certificate. Government jobs are usually strict in this regard.


A provisional certificate only declares that the student has completed their degree and is waiting for their certificate to arrive. It has nothing to do with the physical relocation of a student. Also, unlike a migration certificate, a provisional certificate does not serve any record-keeping purposes.
